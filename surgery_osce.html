History Taking + Follow-Up Written Station

15 站 · 取病史 4 min + 提问 1 min
Format

General Format · 通用考站结构

History Taking Station — Instruction to candidates

Take a focused history from the patient who is just admitted to the surgical ward via the AED and then answer the questions at the next station. Components:

  1. Personal details
  2. Chief complaint
  3. Past medical history
  4. General health status (weight, height, appetite, bowel habit, constitutional)
  5. Social history

Total 10 marks. Usually 0.5 marks per point. Follow-up written station 5 minutes.

Marking scheme (考官参照)

PerformanceMarks
Total disaster, couldn't figure out what to do1
Barely get crucial history, particularly of HPI2
Marginally failed situation3
Satisfactory but not very proficient4
Excellent junior MO level5
Surgical specialist level6

Pass mark = 4. 3 considered failure.

2012 Med 5/3 Hepatobiliary Obstructive Jaundice 阻塞性黄疸

Obstructive Jaundice · 黄疸 (Pancreatic head CA)

Scenario

Take a focused history from the patient referred to the surgical OPD. The patient will also ask you some questions.

Patient script (surrogate)

  • 黄大城, M/75, 退休地盘工人 (15 years), c/o 皮肤变黄 × 3 个月
  • HPI: 越黄越深, 无发烧; 偶有右上腹隐隐痛, 食欲下降; 2 周前小便变 "茶色"; 2 个月口味变差, 近 4 周体重↓~15 lb
  • PMH: 缺血性心脏病, 服阿斯匹灵, 现在无心绞痛; 无肝炎史
  • DH: Aspirin; 对 Augmentin 安灭菌 过敏
  • SH: 吸烟 45 年 (1 包/日), 1 年前因心脏病戒烟; 间中饮啤酒, 一月一次
  • FH: 无肝癌或黄疸

Surrogate 必问的两个问题 (4-min mark)

  1. 想知道点解皮肤变黄？ Obstructive jaundice (2 分 = 满分; 1 分 = 不够清楚)
  2. 如果需要做检查, 想知道呢啲检查会唔会有风险？ 影像 & 血液测试风险不大, 但 ERCP 有一定风险 (2 / 1 分)

Follow-Up Written Station

  1. What is your diagnosis? Pancreatic head cancer 胰头癌
  2. With understanding of physiology, explain why does the skin turn yellow in simple English? Obstructive jaundice — CBD compressed by pancreatic head tumour
  3. USG shows pancreatic head mass; what other signs would you ask radiologist to tell you? Dilated CBD, distended gallbladder (Courvoisier's law)
Courvoisier's Law: painless jaundice + palpable gallbladder ⇒ not gallstones (most likely pancreatic head CA / cholangioCA).
2010 Med 5 Hepatobiliary Chronic Pancreatitis 慢性胰腺炎

Epigastric pain · Chronic Pancreatitis

Scenario

Take a history from a man presented to the surgical OPD with abdominal pain.

History

  • 40/M, bartender, c/o abdominal pain × 1 year
  • Pain in epigastrium, exacerbated by eating, radiates to back, relieved by OTC analgesics
  • Weight loss 20 lb
  • Chronic smoker & drinker; lives alone; father died of HCC

Diagnosis

Chronic pancreatitis (alcoholic etiology)

Classic triad of chronic pancreatitis: epigastric pain radiating to back + steatorrhoea + diabetes. Risk factors mnemonic TIGAR-O (Toxic-metabolic / Idiopathic / Genetic / Autoimmune / Recurrent acute / Obstructive).
2009 Med 5 UGI Gastric Outlet Obstruction 幽门梗阻

Colicky epigastric pain · Gastric Outlet Obstruction

Scenario

Patient with colicky epigastric pain. Change of bowel habit and haematemesis. Partial gastrectomy done.

Follow-Up Written Station

  1. What are the symptoms? Postprandial vomiting (undigested food), early satiety, weight loss, succussion splash
  2. State one blood test to correlate the symptoms. ABG → hypochloraemic hypokalaemic metabolic alkalosis
  3. Differential diagnoses? Gastric CA, peptic ulcer scarring, pancreatic head CA invasion, GIST
  4. Other surgically-treatable DDx? Pyloric stenosis 2° to PUD, malignant infiltration
  5. How does the surgical history correlate with the chief complaint? Recurrent disease at anastomotic site / post-gastrectomy stenosis
2017, 2011 Med 5 · 2016, 2011, 2009 Med 3 UGI CA Oesophagus 食管癌 / Achalasia 贲门失弛缓

Progressive Dysphagia · 进行性吞咽困难

Scenario (surrogate "吳樂格")

  • M/70, painless progressive dysphagia × 6 weeks
  • Tolerate congee, not solids
  • LOW 10 lb in 3 weeks
  • Hypertension, emphysema; on anti-HT drugs
  • Smoker 50 yrs, 1 pack/d, quit 2 yrs ago; 1–2 bottles Chinese wine/day
  • Retired civil servant/clerk

Surrogate instruction: 不问不答. Must elicit each point yourself.

Follow-Up Written 2016 (Barium swallow + OGD shown)

  1. Physical signs to look for? (2) Cachexia, hydration; metastases — hepatomegaly, bone tenderness, Virchow's node (left supraclavicular LN)
  2. 4 risk factors? (4) Smoking, drinking, achalasia, preserved/salted food, family history
  3. Investigation shown + findings? (2+2) Barium swallow — irregular narrowing of oesophagus (apple-core)
  4. Most likely diagnosis? (1) CA oesophagus
  5. Treatment advice? (1) Confirm dx (OGD + biopsy) → stage (EUS, CT, PET-CT) → curative (oesophagectomy + lymphadenectomy) vs palliative (stent, RT, chemo)

Follow-Up Written 2011

  1. DDx (1 mark each): CA oesophagus, CA cardia, motility disorder (achalasia)
  2. Investigation of choice (1 mark each): Barium swallow (NOT water-soluble — causes severe chemical pneumonitis if aspirated), OGD

Follow-Up Written 2009 (Bird's beak barium shown)

  1. What is this? Barium swallow — bird's beak sign
  2. Diagnosis? Achalasia 贲门失弛缓
  3. Other methods to confirm? Manometry
  4. Expected manometric findings? LES fails to relax on wet swallow (<75 % relaxation); LES pressure >100 mmHg = achalasia, >200 = nutcracker; aperistalsis in body; relative ↑ intraoesophageal pressure vs intragastric
⚠ Use barium, not Gastrografin, for suspected obstruction without leak — water-soluble contrast aspirated → fatal pneumonitis.
2016 Med 5 LGI Acute Appendicitis 急性阑尾炎

RLQ Pain · Acute Appendicitis

Scenario

19/F presented with right lower quadrant pain.

History

  • Pain × 1–2 days, initially central → migrated to RLQ, increasing intensity, constant, 6/10
  • No specific aggravating/relieving factors
  • Low grade fever; nausea but no vomiting; no diarrhoea
  • LMP end of last month
  • NSND, NKDA, good past health, FH unremarkable

Examiner's questions

  1. Diagnosis? Acute appendicitis
  2. Investigation? Pregnancy test (β-hCG), CBC, CRP, ESR, septic workup; USG/CT if uncertain
  3. Treatment? Resuscitation, monitor vitals; IV antibiotics (Ampicillin + Cefuroxime + Metronidazole); appendicectomy — laparoscopic vs open
Alvarado score (MANTRELS): Migration, Anorexia, Nausea, Tenderness RLQ, Rebound, Elevated temp, Leucocytosis, Shift to left. ≥7 → high probability.
2007 Med 5 LGI CA Colon · 大肠癌

Blood in Stool (王強 case)

Patient script (王強)

  • M/55, born in Mainland, in HK ~30 yrs, 菜販 in 沙田街市
  • 已婚, 妻 (酒樓洗碗工人), 兩子女 (25 地盤 / 17 中六)
  • SH: 吸烟 30 年 1 包/日; 偶飲啤酒; 每日工作十幾小時, 無時間運動; 鍾意食煎炸食物
  • HPI: 肚痛、肚漲、無大便 5 日 → AED 入院; 半年前起有便秘問題, 3 日一次; 大便最近變幼, 帶血絲及黏液 (黏液好似鼻涕); 2 個月體重↓10 磅; 入院前一星期無胃口、無精神
  • PMH: 1997 HT + ↑cholesterol 控制中; 1980 — 盲腸炎灌膿 (Princess Margaret Hospital), 1982 — 腸塞再開刀 (PMH)
  • FH: 父親 60 歲死於大腸癌; 家姐 48 歲患子宮癌, 切除存活

Follow-Up Written

  1. Main reason for emergency admission? (1) Intestinal obstruction (肚痛 + 肚漲 + 無大便 5 日)
  2. Patient's main problems (with duration) prior to admission? (1.5) Constipation 6 m, change of bowel habit + blood/mucus PR, LOW 10 lb in 2 m, fatigue 1 wk
  3. Risk factors? (2) Family history (1° relatives x2), smoking, diet (fried foods, low fibre)
  4. Clinical significance of father & sister's history? (1.5) Suggest HNPCC/Lynch syndrome — colorectal + endometrial CA at young age
  5. Advice to patient's children? (1) Early surveillance colonoscopy (start age 25 or 10 yrs before youngest affected relative, q1-2 y); consider genetic counselling
  6. Investigation done after admission? Findings? (1.5) Contrast enema (or sigmoidoscopy) — apple-core stricture / mass at recto-sigmoid
  7. Patient asks for laparoscopic surgery — possible? Why? (1.5) Possible in elective setting; in acute obstruction with sepsis/distended bowel — open Hartmann's or resection + table lavage usually safer; assess after decompression
2015 Med 3 LGI FAP · 家族性腺瘤性息肉病

Blood in Stool · Familial Adenomatous Polyposis

Patient info (recall)

  • CC: Blood in stool; fresh, few months, abdominal cramping, sudden LOW, LOA
  • PMH: GPH · DH: Penicillin allergy · FH: Dad & grandpa with CA colon · SH: chronic smoker, social drinker
  • Patient's question: 「醫生, 我究竟咩事呀？」

Marking key points

  • Self-introduction · Consent · CC · Associated symptoms · PMH · DH · FH · SH

Follow-Up Written (colonoscopy showing multiple polyps)

  1. Most likely diagnosis? CA colon
  2. 3 signs in P/E? Pallor, palpable abdominal mass, rectal mass, cervical LN, hepatomegaly, jaundice, ascites (peritoneal mets), pleural effusion (lung mets)
  3. 2 risk factors for CA colon? Smoking, positive family history
  4. Investigation shown (Fig 1, 2)? Colonoscopy
  5. Finding? Multiple polyps / polyposis
  6. Diagnosis based on history + findings? Familial Adenomatous Polyposis (FAP) — APC gene, 5q21, AD, ~100 % cancer risk by age 40, requires prophylactic proctocolectomy
2015, 2008 Med 5 Vascular Leriche Syndrome 主髂动脉闭塞综合征

Bilateral leg + buttock pain · Leriche Syndrome

Scenario

40-yr-old gentleman referred to surgical OPD with 1-year history of bilateral leg pain and buttock pain.

Surrogate (巫能, M/44)

  • Previously construction worker, unemployed × 6 m; recently divorced (jobless + sexual impotence); 1 son, 10 yrs; lives in public estate, poor social support
  • Chronic smoker 25 y, 1–2 packs/d; chronic drinker 25 y, beer; known HT + hyperlipidaemia, FU PWH, poor compliance; loves fried/fatty food
  • c/o bilateral buttock + thigh pain × 1 yr, also bilateral leg pain × 6 m
  • Pain after walking 5 min on level ground; worse upstairs; relieved by standing still; no rest pain, no ulcer
  • Erectile dysfunction × 6 m (don't mention unless asked)

Follow-Up Written

  1. Name of syndrome? (1) Leriche syndrome
  2. Anatomical level + pathophysiology? (2) Aortoiliac occlusive disease 2° to atherosclerosis
  3. 3 risk factors? (2) Chronic smoking (1), HT, hyperlipidaemia (0.5 each)
  4. Typical P/E finding? (1) Absent bilateral femoral pulses
  5. Reasons for divorce? (2) Sexual impotence + jobless
  6. One investigation? (1) Angiogram (CTA / DSA / MRA)
  7. One surgical option? (1) Aortobifemoral bypass
Leriche triad: claudication of buttocks/thighs + impotence + absent femoral pulses.
2014, 2009, 2005 Med 5 · 2009 Med 3 Vascular PVD 周围血管病

Calf pain + gangrenous toes · Peripheral Vascular Disease

Scenario

Patient c/o calf pain; photos showing erythematous foot with gangrenous toes.

Approach

  • HPI — calf pain:
    • Intermittent claudication: muscle pain only on exercise, gone with rest
    • Claudication distance (how far before stopping)
    • Rest pain: hangs legs out of bed at night (severity)
    • r/o DDx: DVT, MSK, infection, trauma, sciatica
  • Risk factors: smoking, DM, HT, HL
  • PMH

Follow-Up Written

  1. P/E? PVD exam — inspection (atrophic skin, hair loss, ulcer, gangrene), palpation (temp, cap refill, pulses DP/PT/Pop/Fem), Buerger's test (pallor + Buerger's angle), ABPI <0.9 abnormal
  2. Treatment? Conservative — smoking cessation, exercise, control DM/HT/HL, foot care · Medical — antiplatelet (aspirin), cilostazol/naftidrofuryl/pentoxifylline · Surgical — angioplasty, bypass graft
Fontaine classification: I asymp · IIa >200m · IIb <200m · III rest pain · IV ulcer/gangrene. ABPI <0.4 = critical limb ischaemia.
2014 Med 5 · 2013 Med 3 Urology CA Bladder 膀胱癌

Painless Haematuria · Bladder Cancer

Chief complaint

Painless haematuria. All ROS negative except smoking and occupation (dye factory).

Follow-Up Written

  1. Diagnosis? Bladder cancer / urinary tract cancer
  2. Occupational risk factor most concerning? Hairdresser, dye/rubber/leather factory — aromatic amines (β-naphthylamine, benzidine)
  3. 2 relevant P/E? Pallor; abdominal mass (mass itself / enlarged LN / liver mets); palpation of supraclavicular fossa (Virchow's)
  4. Relevant investigations & how? Flexible cystoscopy — aseptic, LA + lubricant, into urethral meatus, clear view inside bladder; also urine cytology, CT urogram
  5. If haematuria now associated with pain — change DDx? Why? No — UTI can coexist with CA bladder; still need workup
2014, 2010 Med 3 Urology BPH 良性前列腺增生 / CA Prostate 前列腺癌

Difficulty Passing Urine · BPH / Prostate Cancer

Scenario

65/M with difficulty in passing urine.

Marked by surrogate (they are nice; most scored 7 or 8). Take full history; leave time for questions. Common surrogate worry: prognosis.

Follow-Up Written

  1. Most worrying diagnosis? (2) CA prostate
  2. Other conditions that ↑ PSA? (2) BPH, prostatitis, UTI (also recent DRE, ejaculation, urinary retention, cycling)
  3. 2 P/E findings to look for? (2) DRE: size, shape, consistency of prostate, presence of median groove (lost in CA), nodules
  4. Most important investigation to confirm diagnosis? (1) TRUS + biopsy
  5. 3 complications of TRUS biopsy? (2) Haematuria, haematospermia, PR bleeding, UTI, sepsis, urinary retention
  6. If CA prostate confirmed — 2 further investigations? (1) CT, bone scan, MRI; CXR; ?PET-CT

⚠ If Q4 wrong, you lose downstream marks.

2012, 2010, 2007, 2005 Med 5 · 2009, 2008 Med 3 O&T Sciatica · L5/S1 PID

Low Back Pain · Sciatica (Prolapsed Intervertebral Disc)

Scenario

40-yr-old lady with low back pain after moving a sofa 1 week ago. The pain is associated with numbness over left lateral leg and weakness of big toe.

Key history points (16-item template)

  1. Acute onset 1 wk ago, lifting heavy furniture; sudden severe pain (8/10) at lumbosacral junction, electrifying/tingling
  2. First episode; severity same all week; worse evenings; still able to sleep
  3. Increased with sitting, squatting, bending forward, coughing; better with standing; best supine
  4. Worse on exertion, better with rest
  5. Radiates to lateral border + sole of left foot, with numbness over same site
  6. No sphincter disturbance; left big toe felt weak
  7. No red flags: fever, weight loss, anorexia, trauma, night sweats, joint pain, TB contact, steroid use, cancer history
  8. Retired teacher; spouse + lives at home; non-smoker, non-drinker
  9. Expectation: "just want to get rid of the pain"; tried Panadol — not useful; mood OK

Diagnosis required at 4.5/5.5-min mark

  • ✓ PID, spinal stenosis (2 marks each)
  • ✓ Spondylolisthesis, degenerative spine disease (1 mark)
  • ✗ TB spine, spinal metastasis, osteoporotic vertebral collapse (no mark — no red flags)
Red flags for back pain (TUNAFISH): Trauma · Unexplained weight loss · Neurological deficit · Age >50 or <20 · Fever · IV drug use / Immunosuppression · Steroids · History of cancer.
2014, 2011 Med 5 O&T Osteosarcoma 骨肉瘤

Right distal medial thigh pain · Osteosarcoma

Scenario

A patient complains of right distal medial thigh pain.

Key history to elicit

  • Pain — site (around knee, distal femur metaphysis), character (constant, worse at night), duration
  • Swelling, lump growing
  • Constitutional: weight loss, fever, fatigue
  • Loss of function, limp
  • FH: retinoblastoma (Rb gene), Li-Fraumeni syndrome (p53)
  • PMH: previous Paget's disease, radiation, retinoblastoma
Osteosarcoma — bimodal age peak (adolescents + elderly with Paget's). Sites: distal femur (most common), proximal tibia, proximal humerus. X-ray: Codman's triangle, sunburst periosteal reaction. Mets to lung.
2009 Med 5 O&T Cervical Myelopathy 颈髓病

Cervical Myelopathy

Key features to elicit

  • Neck pain, stiffness
  • UMN signs below lesion: clumsy hands, difficulty with fine motor (buttons, chopsticks), unsteady gait, falls
  • Sensory: glove-and-stocking or dermatomal
  • Sphincter disturbance (late, ominous)
  • Lhermitte's sign: electric shock down spine on neck flexion
Hoffmann's sign, hyperreflexia below level + hyporeflexia at level — cervical myelopathy. MRI is investigation of choice.
